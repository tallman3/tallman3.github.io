Canadian Income Alternatives - Large Cap Dividend Funds

Challenging Times

For several decades, many Canadians have used Canada Premium Bonds (CPBs) and Canada Savings Bonds (CSBs) to generate income during retirement with a low risk profile. Unfortunately in the current low-interest environment, it is no longer a viable retirement plan. Although the Canadian stock market, as represented by the TSE index, has been volatile and lost almost 35% in one year during the 2008 recession, older investors would be far better off today if they had invested in the stock market rather than government bonds.

Did You Know?

There is no broad consensus in Canada on the true measurement of a Savings or Investment rate.

  • In this analysis, the Canadian Premium Bonds (CPBs) savings rate are used to represent savings. CPBs are widely available to most Canadians with a competitive interest rate.
  • The Toronto Stock Exchange index (TSE) will be used to represent a return on investment. The TSE represents the majority of Canadian public companies and is a good representative for typical Canadian investors.
  • Canada's inflation is measured the the Consumer Price Index (CPI)
